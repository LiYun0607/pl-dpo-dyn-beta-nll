Abstract

Research Summary

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models have shown great potential for end-to-end autonomous driving, yet aligning them with safety constraints remains a challenge due to long-tail data imbalance and probability collapse, a phenomenon where the likelihood of safe actions degrades during preference optimization. To bridge this gap, we propose PL-DPO-NLL, a safety-aware preference learning framework. We introduce three key contributions: (1) Plackett-Luce multi-preference ranking, which extends pairwise comparisons to risk-ranked trajectory lists to capture fine-grained safety severities; (2) Scene-adaptive β weighting, which dynamically amplifies optimization gradients in safety-critical scenarios; and (3) NLL regularization, which explicitly anchors the policy to expert demonstrations to prevent probability collapse. On the CARLA closed-loop benchmark, our method achieves a 58.26 Driving Score (+8.4% over baseline), setting a new state-of-the-art in route completion (65.9%) and infraction penalty (89.1%) compared to existing DPO variants (IPO, EXO, BCO, SPPO).

Motivation

Why PL-DPO-NLL?

Three critical challenges in preference-based driving and our solutions.

Challenge 1: Probability Collapse

The Pain: Standard DPO maximizes relative margins but ignores absolute probability. The model loses confidence in safe actions during training (green curve → flat curve).

Our Fix: NLL Regularization (+4.4%)
We anchor the policy to expert demonstrations, ensuring high confidence in safety-critical decisions.

Challenge 2: Beyond Binary Choice

The Pain: Real driving isn't just "Safe vs. Unsafe". It's a spectrum: Safe > Risky > Critical. Pairwise comparisons lose this information.

Our Fix: Plackett-Luce Ranking (+4.0%)
We learn from ranked trajectory lists, capturing fine-grained safety severities.

Challenge 3: Long-Tail Imbalance

The Pain: Critical scenarios (red lights, pedestrians) are rare (<6% of data), leading to underfitting on exactly the situations where safety matters most.

Our Fix: Scene-Adaptive β (+1.0%)
We dynamically amplify gradients (up to 2.9×) for life-critical moments.

  Core Formulations

Plackett-Luce DPO Loss

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{PL-DPO}} = -\mathbb{E}_{(x, \mathcal{Y})} \left[ \sum_{i=1}^{M} \log \frac{\exp(\beta \cdot r_i)}{\sum_{j=i}^{M} \exp(\beta \cdot r_j)} \right]\]

where \(r_i = \log \frac{\pi_\theta(y^{(i)}|x)}{\pi_{\text{ref}}(y^{(i)}|x)}\) is the implicit reward, and \(M = K + 1\) candidates are ranked by risk severity.

NLL Regularization

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{NLL}} = -\log \pi_\theta(y_w|x)\]

Directly maximizes the probability of expert (chosen) actions, preventing the probability collapse phenomenon observed in vanilla DPO.

Combined Objective

\[\mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{PL-DPO}} + \lambda \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{NLL}}\]

The hyperparameter \(\lambda = 0.1\) provides optimal balance between preference learning and likelihood maximization.

Gradient Amplification

\[\frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \theta} \propto \beta \cdot \nabla_\theta \log \frac{\pi_\theta(y_w | x)}{\pi_\theta(y_l | x)}\]

Scene-adaptive \(\beta\) (0.35 vs 0.12) amplifies gradients for safety-critical scenarios by 2.9×, despite comprising only 5.8% of data.

Performance Comparison Analysis

We compare PL-DPO-NLL against six state-of-the-art preference learning methods. Key observations:

  • Driving Score: PL-DPO-NLL achieves 58.26, an 8.41% improvement over SFT baseline (53.74). Standard DPO only improves by 3.52%.
  • Route Completion: Our method reaches 65.9%, 2.9 percentage points higher than baseline. NLL regularization prevents "forgetting" of successful driving patterns.
  • Safety (Infraction Penalty): With 0.891, our method has the highest penalty score (= fewest violations). EXO achieves similar safety (0.899) but lower driving score.
  • Ablation insight: PL-DPO alone achieves +3.96%, adding NLL regularization contributes an additional +4.45%, demonstrating their synergy.

Key Takeaways

  1. NLL regularization is critical: It provides the largest single-component improvement (+2.39 driving score) by preventing probability collapse.
  2. Multi-preference ranking matters: Plackett-Luce formulation outperforms all pairwise DPO variants (DPO, IPO, EXO, BCO, SPPO).
  3. Scene-adaptive β improves safety: Dynamic weighting reduces red light violations by 28.05% (0.82 â†’ 0.59) compared to fixed β.
  4. Components are synergistic: The combination achieves 8.4% total improvement, greater than the sum of individual gains.
